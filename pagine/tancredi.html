  • Rossini's "Tancredi"

    The publishing case of Gioacchino Rossini's "Tancredi" takes the form of a peculiar alternation of stagings and endings over the course of a very short span of time: set in Syracuse in 1005 and written, the opera and libretto edited by Gaetano Rossi tells of the tormented love affair between the exiled knight Tancredi and the noblewoman Amenaide during the wars between the Saracens and the Sicilians.

    The premiere, staged in Venice in February 1813, featured a happy ending marked by an atmosphere of general jubilation at the end of hostilities and the fulfillment of love between Tancredi and Amenaide.

    A little more than a month later, on March 21, 1813, Rossini in collaboration with librettist Luigi Lechi staged a second edition in Ferrara that included a tragic ending, proposing Tancredi's death: the audience did not like the new ending, and for this reason in December of the same year a third edition of Tancredi was staged in Milan, re-presenting the first ending with the inclusion of some additions.
